## Question

A solid cone has base radius 5 cm and slant height 13 cm.

(a) Find the perpendicular height of the cone.

(b) Find the volume of the cone, giving your answer correct to 3 significant figures.

(c) Find the total surface area of the cone, giving your answer correct to 3 significant figures.

[Take \(\pi = 3.142\). Volume of a cone \(= \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\). Curved surface area of a cone \(= \pi r l\).]

## Solution

**Step 1 — (a) Find the height using Pythagoras' theorem.**

The radius, height, and slant height form a right-angled triangle, with the slant height as the hypotenuse:

$$h = \sqrt{l^2 - r^2} = \sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{169 - 25} = \sqrt{144} = 12 \text{ cm}$$

**Step 2 — (b) Find the volume.**

$$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3} \times \pi \times 5^2 \times 12 = 100\pi$$

$$V = 100 \times 3.142 = 314.2 \approx 314 \text{ cm}^3 \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Step 3 — (c) Find the total surface area (curved surface + base circle).**

$$A = \pi r l + \pi r^2 = \pi(5)(13) + \pi(5)^2 = 65\pi + 25\pi = 90\pi$$

$$A = 90 \times 3.142 = 282.78 \approx 283 \text{ cm}^2 \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Check:** using the more precise value \(\pi = 3.14159\ldots\), \(100\pi = 314.159\ldots \to 314\) cm³ and \(90\pi = 282.743\ldots \to 283\) cm², confirming the roundings in Steps 2 and 3 are correct to 3 s.f.

**Common mistake:** using the slant height \(l\) in place of the perpendicular height \(h\) in the volume formula (\(V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\) needs the *perpendicular* height, not the slant height) — this is the single most common error in cone mensuration questions.

## Answer

(a) \(h = 12\) cm. (b) \(V = 314\) cm³ (3 s.f.). (c) Total surface area \(= 283\) cm² (3 s.f.).
