## Question

A car travels a distance of 240 km at an average speed of \(x\) km/h. If the average speed had been 20 km/h greater, the journey would have taken 1 hour less.

1. Form an equation in \(x\) and show that it simplifies to \(x^2 + 20x - 4800 = 0\).
2. Solve the equation to find the original average speed.

## Solution

**Part 1 — Form the equation.**

Time taken at the original speed: \(\dfrac{240}{x}\) hours.

Time taken at the faster speed: \(\dfrac{240}{x + 20}\) hours.

The faster journey takes 1 hour less:

$$\frac{240}{x} - \frac{240}{x+20} = 1$$

Multiply every term by \(x(x+20)\):

$$240(x+20) - 240x = x(x+20)$$

$$240x + 4800 - 240x = x^2 + 20x$$

$$x^2 + 20x - 4800 = 0$$

**Part 2 — Solve.** Factorise: we need two numbers that multiply to \(-4800\) and add to \(20\); these are \(80\) and \(-60\).

$$(x + 80)(x - 60) = 0$$

So \(x = -80\) or \(x = 60\).

**Reject** \(x = -80\) because speed cannot be negative.

## Answer

The original average speed is **60 km/h**.
