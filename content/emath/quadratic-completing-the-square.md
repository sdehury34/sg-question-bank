## Question

Solve the equation \(x^2 - 6x + 2 = 0\) by completing the square, giving your answers correct to 3 significant figures.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Move the constant to the right-hand side.**

$$x^2 - 6x = -2$$

**Step 2 — Complete the square.** Take half the coefficient of \(x\), which is \(-3\), and add its square \((-3)^2 = 9\) to both sides:

$$x^2 - 6x + 9 = -2 + 9$$

$$(x - 3)^2 = 7$$

**Step 3 — Square root both sides.** Remember the \(\pm\):

$$x - 3 = \pm\sqrt{7}$$

**Step 4 — Solve for \(x\).**

$$x = 3 + \sqrt{7} \quad \text{or} \quad x = 3 - \sqrt{7}$$

Evaluating: \(\sqrt{7} \approx 2.6458\), so \(x \approx 5.6458\) or \(x \approx 0.35425\).

**Common mistake:** forgetting the \(\pm\) when square-rooting, which loses one of the two solutions.

## Answer

\(x = 5.65\) or \(x = 0.354\) (3 s.f.)
