## Question

A uniform metre rule of weight 1.2 N is pivoted at its 50 cm mark (its centre) and balances horizontally. A weight of 3.0 N is hung from the 20 cm mark.

(a) State the moment of the rule's own weight about the pivot, with a reason.

(b) A second weight of 4.0 N is now hung on the other side of the pivot. At what mark on the rule must it be placed so that the rule remains balanced?

## Solution

**Part (a).**

Since the rule is uniform, its weight acts at its centre of mass, which is the 50 cm mark — exactly where the pivot is. The perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of this weight is therefore zero, so:

$$\text{moment} = \text{force} \times \text{distance} = 1.2 \times 0 = 0 \text{ N m}$$

The rule's own weight contributes **no moment** about the pivot, regardless of its 1.2 N value.

**Part (b).**

Distance of the 3.0 N weight from the pivot:

$$d_1 = 50 - 20 = 30 \text{ cm} = 0.30 \text{ m}$$

Its moment about the pivot:

$$M_1 = 3.0 \times 0.30 = 0.90 \text{ N m}$$

By the principle of moments, for equilibrium the 4.0 N weight (on the opposite side of the pivot) must produce an equal and opposite moment:

$$4.0 \times d_2 = 0.90 \implies d_2 = \frac{0.90}{4.0} = 0.225 \text{ m} = 22.5 \text{ cm}$$

Since the 3.0 N weight is on the left of the pivot (20 cm mark), the 4.0 N weight must go on the **right**, at:

$$50 + 22.5 = 72.5 \text{ cm}$$

**Check:** moment of 3.0 N weight = \(3.0 \times 30 = 90\) N cm (say, anticlockwise); moment of 4.0 N weight = \(4.0 \times 22.5 = 90\) N cm (clockwise). The two moments are equal and opposite, so the rule balances. ✓

**Common mistake:** including the rule's own weight in the moment equation with a non-zero arm (e.g. taking its distance from one end of the rule instead of from the pivot). Because the rule is uniform and pivoted exactly at its centre of mass, its weight passes through the pivot and contributes zero moment — it does add to the total downward force supported at the pivot, but it does not affect where the 4.0 N weight must be placed.

## Answer

(a) 0 N m — the rule's weight acts at the pivot itself, so its moment arm is zero.

(b) The 4.0 N weight must be hung at the **72.5 cm mark** (22.5 cm from the pivot, on the opposite side to the 3.0 N weight).
