## Question

A 6 Ω resistor and a 3 Ω resistor are connected in parallel. This combination is connected in series with a 4 Ω resistor across a 12 V battery of negligible internal resistance. Find

1. the effective resistance of the circuit,
2. the current drawn from the battery,
3. the current through the 6 Ω resistor.

## Solution

**Part 1 — Effective resistance.** For the parallel pair:

$$\frac{1}{R_p} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_p = 2\ \Omega$$

Total resistance (series): \(R = R_p + 4 = 2 + 4 = 6\ \Omega\)

**Part 2 — Battery current.** Using \(I = \dfrac{V}{R}\):

$$I = \frac{12}{6} = 2\ \text{A}$$

**Part 3 — Current through the 6 Ω resistor.** The full 2 A flows through the 4 Ω resistor, so the p.d. across it is \(V_4 = 2 \times 4 = 8\ \text{V}\). The p.d. across the parallel pair is therefore

$$V_p = 12 - 8 = 4\ \text{V}$$

Current through the 6 Ω branch:

$$I_6 = \frac{V_p}{6} = \frac{4}{6} = 0.667\ \text{A (3 s.f.)}$$

**Check:** current through the 3 Ω branch is \(4/3 = 1.33\) A, and \(0.667 + 1.33 = 2\) A = battery current ✓

## Answer

1. Effective resistance = **6 Ω**
2. Current from the battery = **2 A**
3. Current through the 6 Ω resistor = **0.667 A**
