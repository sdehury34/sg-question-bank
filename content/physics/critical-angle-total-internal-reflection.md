## Question

A ray of light travels inside a glass block of refractive index \(n = 1.50\) and strikes the glass–air boundary from inside the glass at an angle of incidence of 45°.

(a) Calculate the critical angle \(C\) for the glass–air boundary.

(b) State, with a reason, whether total internal reflection occurs for this ray.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Critical angle formula.**

The critical angle satisfies \(\sin C = \dfrac{1}{n}\) (light travelling from the denser medium, glass, towards the less dense medium, air):

$$\sin C = \frac{1}{1.50} = 0.667$$

$$C = \sin^{-1}(0.667) = 41.8^\circ \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Step 2 — Compare the angle of incidence to the critical angle.**

The angle of incidence inside the glass is \(45^\circ\), and \(45^\circ > 41.8^\circ = C\).

**Step 3 — Conclusion.**

Total internal reflection requires two conditions: light travelling from a denser to a less dense medium, and the angle of incidence exceeding the critical angle. Both conditions are met here (glass → air, and \(45^\circ > 41.8^\circ\)), so total internal reflection occurs — the ray does not refract into the air and is instead fully reflected back into the glass.

**Check:** \(\sin(41.8^\circ) = 0.666\), which rounds to \(1/1.50 = 0.667\) ✓, confirming the critical angle calculation.

**Common mistake:** writing \(\sin C = n\) instead of \(\sin C = 1/n\), which gives a value greater than 1 and no valid angle — the critical angle formula only works going from the optically denser medium into the less dense one.

## Answer

(a) \(C = 41.8^\circ\).

(b) Yes, total internal reflection occurs, since the angle of incidence (45°) is greater than the critical angle (41.8°).
