## Question

A ball is dropped from rest from the top of a building 45 m tall. Ignoring air resistance and taking the acceleration of free fall as \(g = 10\ \text{m/s}^2\), find

1. the time taken for the ball to reach the ground,
2. the speed of the ball just before it hits the ground.

## Solution

**Set up:** the ball starts from rest, so initial velocity \(u = 0\). Displacement \(s = 45\) m, acceleration \(a = g = 10\ \text{m/s}^2\).

**Part 1 — Time of fall.** Use \(s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2\):

$$45 = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(10)t^2$$

$$45 = 5t^2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad t^2 = 9 \quad\Rightarrow\quad t = 3\ \text{s}$$

(We reject \(t = -3\) s since time must be positive.)

**Part 2 — Impact speed.** Use \(v = u + at\):

$$v = 0 + (10)(3) = 30\ \text{m/s}$$

**Alternative check** using \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\): \(v^2 = 0 + 2(10)(45) = 900\), so \(v = 30\ \text{m/s}\) ✓

## Answer

1. Time to reach the ground = **3 s**
2. Speed just before impact = **30 m/s**
