## Question

Find the equation of the tangent to the curve \(y = x^3 - 4x + 1\) at the point where \(x = 2\).

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the point on the curve.** When \(x = 2\):

$$y = 2^3 - 4(2) + 1 = 8 - 8 + 1 = 1$$

So the tangent touches the curve at \((2, 1)\).

**Step 2 — Differentiate to get the gradient function.**

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 4$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the gradient at \(x = 2\).**

$$\frac{dy}{dx}\Big|_{x=2} = 3(2)^2 - 4 = 12 - 4 = 8$$

**Step 4 — Use the point-gradient form** \(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\):

$$y - 1 = 8(x - 2)$$

$$y = 8x - 15$$

**Check:** at \(x = 2\), \(y = 16 - 15 = 1\) ✓ (passes through the point of tangency).

## Answer

The equation of the tangent is \(y = 8x - 15\).
