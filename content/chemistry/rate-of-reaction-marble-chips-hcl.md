## Question

Excess marble chips (calcium carbonate) are reacted with dilute hydrochloric acid in a conical flask connected to a gas syringe. The volume of carbon dioxide gas collected is recorded every 20 seconds:

| Time / s | 0 | 20 | 40 | 60 | 80 | 100 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume of CO₂ / cm³ | 0 | 24 | 40 | 50 | 56 | 56 |

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction between \( t = 0\text{ s} \) and \( t = 40\text{ s} \), in \( \text{cm}^3\text{s}^{-1} \).

(b) Explain, in terms of collision theory, why the rate of reaction between \( t = 60\text{ s} \) and \( t = 80\text{ s} \) is slower than between \( t = 0\text{ s} \) and \( t = 20\text{ s} \).

(c) State the time at which the reaction is complete, and explain how the data shows this.

## Solution

**(a) Average rate between 0 s and 40 s**

Average rate of reaction is the change in gas volume divided by the change in time:
$$\text{average rate} = \frac{\Delta V}{\Delta t} = \frac{40 - 0}{40 - 0} = \frac{40\text{ cm}^3}{40\text{ s}} = 1.0\text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}$$

**Check:** over the first 20 s the rate was \(24/20 = 1.2\text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}\), and over the next 20 s (20 s to 40 s) it was \((40-24)/20 = 0.8\text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}\). The average of these two 20 s intervals, \((1.2+0.8)/2 = 1.0\text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}\), matches the overall average calculated directly. ✓

**(b) Why the rate slows down**

Marble chips are in excess, so the hydrochloric acid is the reagent being used up as the reaction proceeds. As HCl is consumed, its concentration in the flask decreases, so the number of acid particles per unit volume falls. This lowers the frequency of collisions between HCl particles and the surface of the calcium carbonate, so fewer collisions result in a reaction per second — the rate decreases over time.

**(c) When the reaction is complete**

Comparing the volumes at \( t = 80\text{ s} \) (56 cm³) and \( t = 100\text{ s} \) (56 cm³), no further gas is produced between these two readings — the volume has become constant. This shows the reaction was already complete by \( t = 80\text{ s} \), since gas is only released while the reaction is still occurring.

**Common mistake:** Calculating "the rate at 40 s" by dividing the *final* volume (56 cm³) by the *total* time (100 s), which gives the average rate over the whole reaction (0.56 cm³ s⁻¹), not the rate over the requested 0–40 s interval. Always use the volume change divided by the time change *for the specific interval asked about*.

## Answer

(a) \( 1.0\text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1} \).
(b) Acid concentration falls as it is used up, reducing collision frequency between acid particles and the marble surface, so the rate decreases.
(c) The reaction is complete by \( t = 80\text{ s} \), since the gas volume stays at 56 cm³ from 80 s to 100 s (no more gas is being produced).
