## Question

Excess marble chips (calcium carbonate) are reacted with 50.0 cm³ of 0.400 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid at room temperature and pressure (r.t.p.). The volume of carbon dioxide gas collected is recorded over time:

| Time / s | 0 | 20 | 40 | 60 | 80 | 100 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume of CO₂ / cm³ | 0 | 160 | 216 | 236 | 240 | 240 |

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction, in cm³/s, during the first 20 seconds.

(b) Given that HCl is the limiting reagent and that 1 mole of gas occupies 24 dm³ at r.t.p., calculate the total volume of CO₂ expected, and compare it with the experimental final volume of 240 cm³.

## Solution

**(a) Average rate over the first 20 s.**

$$\text{rate} = \frac{\Delta V}{\Delta t} = \frac{160 - 0}{20 - 0} = 8.00\ \text{cm}^3/\text{s}$$

**(b) Predicting the final gas volume from the limiting reagent.**

The reaction is:

$$\text{CaCO}_3 + 2\text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{CaCl}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{CO}_2$$

Moles of HCl available:

$$n(\text{HCl}) = \frac{50.0}{1000} \times 0.400 = 0.0200\ \text{mol}$$

From the 2 : 1 mole ratio of HCl to CO₂:

$$n(\text{CO}_2) = \frac{0.0200}{2} = 0.0100\ \text{mol}$$

Volume of CO₂ at r.t.p. (using 24 dm³ per mole, **not** the STP value of 22.4 dm³):

$$V(\text{CO}_2) = 0.0100 \times 24 = 0.240\ \text{dm}^3 = 240\ \text{cm}^3$$

This matches the experimental final volume of 240 cm³ exactly, confirming that HCl is indeed the limiting reagent and that the reaction has gone to completion by \(t = 80\) s (the volume stays at 240 cm³ from \(t=80\) s onward).

**Common mistake:** using the STP molar gas volume (22.4 dm³/mol) instead of the r.t.p. value (24 dm³/mol) required by the Singapore syllabus — the two are easy to mix up and give different final answers. Another common error is using a 1 : 1 mole ratio between CaCO₃ and HCl instead of the correct 2 : 1 ratio, which would double the predicted gas volume.

## Answer

(a) Average rate = **8.00 cm³/s**

(b) Predicted volume of CO₂ = **240 cm³**, which matches the experimental final volume, confirming HCl is the limiting reagent.
