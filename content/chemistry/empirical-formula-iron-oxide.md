## Question

An oxide of iron is found to contain 7.0 g of iron combined with 3.0 g of oxygen. Determine the empirical formula of the oxide.

[\( A_r \): Fe = 56, O = 16]

## Solution

Find the number of moles of each element:

$$n(\text{Fe}) = \frac{7.0}{56} = 0.125 \text{ mol}$$

$$n(\text{O}) = \frac{3.0}{16} = 0.1875 \text{ mol}$$

Divide both by the smaller value (0.125) to find the simplest mole ratio:

$$\text{Fe} : \text{O} = \frac{0.125}{0.125} : \frac{0.1875}{0.125} = 1 : 1.5$$

Since 1.5 is not a whole number, multiply both sides by 2 to clear the fraction:

$$\text{Fe} : \text{O} = 2 : 3$$

So the empirical formula is \( \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \).

**Check by percentage composition.** \( M(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3) = 2(56) + 3(16) = 160 \).

$$\%\text{Fe} = \frac{112}{160} \times 100\% = 70.0\%, \qquad \%\text{O} = \frac{48}{160} \times 100\% = 30.0\%$$

The original data gives \( \%\text{Fe} = \dfrac{7.0}{10.0} \times 100\% = 70.0\% \) and \( \%\text{O} = \dfrac{3.0}{10.0} \times 100\% = 30.0\% \), which match exactly. ✓

**Common mistake:** Rounding the mole ratio 1 : 1.5 down to 1 : 2 (giving the wrong formula \( \text{FeO}_2 \)) instead of multiplying *both* parts of the ratio by 2 to clear the fraction correctly, which gives 2 : 3.

## Answer

The empirical formula is \( \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \) (iron(III) oxide).
