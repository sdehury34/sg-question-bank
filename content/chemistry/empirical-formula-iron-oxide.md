## Question

An oxide of iron is found to contain 70.0% iron and 30.0% oxygen by mass. \([A_r: \text{Fe} = 56, \text{O} = 16]\)

Determine the empirical formula of this oxide.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Assume a 100 g sample**, so the percentages become masses directly:

- Mass of Fe = 70.0 g
- Mass of O = 30.0 g

**Step 2 — Convert each mass to moles** using \(n = \dfrac{m}{M_r}\):

$$n(\text{Fe}) = \frac{70.0}{56} = 1.25 \text{ mol}$$

$$n(\text{O}) = \frac{30.0}{16} = 1.875 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 3 — Divide by the smallest number of moles** (1.25) to find the simplest mole ratio:

$$\text{Fe} : \text{O} = \frac{1.25}{1.25} : \frac{1.875}{1.25} = 1 : 1.5$$

**Step 4 — Multiply through by 2** to obtain whole-number ratios:

$$\text{Fe} : \text{O} = 2 : 3$$

**Check:** for \(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3\) (\(M_r = 2(56) + 3(16) = 112 + 48 = 160\)):

$$\%\text{Fe} = \frac{112}{160} \times 100\% = 70.0\% \qquad \%\text{O} = \frac{48}{160} \times 100\% = 30.0\%$$

Both match the given percentages, confirming the formula.

**Common mistake:** dividing by the largest mole value instead of the smallest, or stopping at the ratio \(1 : 1.5\) without scaling up to whole numbers — an empirical formula must use the smallest whole-number ratio, so \(\text{Fe}_{1}\text{O}_{1.5}\) must become \(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3\).

## Answer

The empirical formula is **Fe₂O₃**.
