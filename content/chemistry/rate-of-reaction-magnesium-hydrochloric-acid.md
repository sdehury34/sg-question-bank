## Question

0.24 g of magnesium ribbon is added to 50 cm³ of 2.0 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid, which is in excess. All the magnesium dissolves after 40 s, releasing hydrogen gas.

$$\text{Mg}(s) + 2\text{HCl}(aq) \rightarrow \text{MgCl}_2(aq) + \text{H}_2(g)$$

[\(A_r\): Mg = 24. Molar volume of gas at r.t.p. = 24000 cm³/mol.]

(a) Show that hydrochloric acid is indeed in excess.

(b) Calculate the total volume of hydrogen gas collected at r.t.p.

(c) Calculate the average rate of reaction, in cm³/s.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Moles of each reactant.**

$$n(\text{Mg}) = \frac{0.24}{24} = 0.01 \text{ mol}$$

$$n(\text{HCl}) = 2.0 \times \frac{50}{1000} = 0.1 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 2 — Show HCl is in excess.**

From the equation, 0.01 mol Mg requires \(2 \times 0.01 = 0.02\) mol HCl. Since 0.1 mol HCl is available and 0.1 mol > 0.02 mol, HCl is in excess and Mg is the limiting reagent.

**Step 3 — Moles and volume of H₂ produced.**

The Mg : H₂ mole ratio is 1 : 1, so:

$$n(\text{H}_2) = n(\text{Mg}) = 0.01 \text{ mol}$$

$$V(\text{H}_2) = 0.01 \times 24000 = 240 \text{ cm}^3$$

**Step 4 — Average rate of reaction.**

$$\text{average rate} = \frac{\text{volume of gas produced}}{\text{time taken}} = \frac{240}{40} = 6 \text{ cm}^3/\text{s}$$

**Common mistake:** using the mole ratio for HCl (1 : 2) instead of Mg (1 : 1) when finding moles of H₂, since Mg — not HCl — is the limiting reagent here.

## Answer

(a) 0.1 mol HCl available > 0.02 mol HCl needed, so HCl is in excess.

(b) 240 cm³ of H₂ is collected.

(c) The average rate of reaction is **6 cm³/s**.
