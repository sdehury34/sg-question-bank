## Question

Excess calcium carbonate chips are reacted with 50 cm³ of 2.0 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid. The volume of carbon dioxide gas collected is recorded every 10 seconds:

| Time / s | 0 | 10 | 20 | 30 | 40 | 50 | 60 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume of CO₂ / cm³ | 0 | 480 | 800 | 1020 | 1150 | 1200 | 1200 |

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction during the first 10 seconds.

(b) Using the mole concept, show that the final volume of gas (1200 cm³) is consistent with the data, and state the time at which the reaction is complete. (1 mole of gas occupies 24000 cm³ at room temperature and pressure.)

## Solution

**Part (a).**

$$\text{average rate} = \frac{\text{change in volume}}{\text{change in time}} = \frac{480 - 0}{10 - 0} = 48 \text{ cm}^3/\text{s}$$

**Part (b).**

**Step 1 — Moles of HCl (the limiting reagent, since CaCO₃ is in excess).**

$$n(\text{HCl}) = \frac{50}{1000} \times 2.0 = 0.10 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 2 — Reaction equation and mole ratio.**

$$\text{CaCO}_3 + 2\text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{CaCl}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{CO}_2$$

From the equation, 2 mol HCl produces 1 mol CO₂:

$$n(\text{CO}_2) = \frac{0.10}{2} = 0.05 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 3 — Convert to volume at r.t.p.**

$$V(\text{CO}_2) = 0.05 \times 24000 = 1200 \text{ cm}^3$$

This matches the plateau value of 1200 cm³ read from the table, confirming the data is consistent with the mole calculation.

**Step 4 — Identify when the reaction is complete.** The volume stops increasing between \(t=50\text{ s}\) and \(t=60\text{ s}\) (both read 1200 cm³), so the reaction is complete by \(t = 50\text{ s}\).

**Common mistake:** calculating the "rate" using the final total volume divided by the final total time (\(1200 \div 60 = 20\text{ cm}^3/\text{s}\)) instead of the volume change over the specific interval asked for — this gives the average rate over the *whole* reaction, not the first 10 seconds, and the reaction rate is not constant since it slows down as acid is used up.

## Answer

(a) 48 cm³/s. (b) The mole calculation gives 1200 cm³ of CO₂, matching the graph; the reaction is complete by **t = 50 s**.
