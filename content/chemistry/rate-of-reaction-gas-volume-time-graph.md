## Question

Excess dilute hydrochloric acid is added to small marble chips (calcium carbonate) in a conical flask connected to a gas syringe. The volume of carbon dioxide gas collected, \(V\), is recorded every 20 seconds:

| Time \(t\) / s | 0 | 20 | 40 | 60 | 80 | 100 | 120 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume \(V\) / cm³ | 0 | 32 | 52 | 64 | 70 | 72 | 72 |

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction between \(t = 20\text{ s}\) and \(t = 60\text{ s}\), in cm³ s⁻¹.

(b) State, with a reason, the time at which the reaction stops.

(c) Explain, in terms of particle collisions, why the rate of reaction decreases as time goes on.

## Solution

**(a) Average rate between \(t=20\) s and \(t=60\) s.** Average rate is the *change* in volume over that interval, divided by the time taken — not the total volume divided by total time:
$$\text{average rate} = \frac{V(60) - V(20)}{60 - 20} = \frac{64 - 32}{40} = \frac{32}{40} = 0.8 \text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1}$$

**Check.** Reading the table directly: between 20 s and 60 s, 32 cm³ of gas was produced (\(64-32\)) over 40 s, giving \(32/40 = 0.8\) cm³ s⁻¹, consistent with the calculation.

**(b) When the reaction stops.** The volume stays constant at 72 cm³ from \(t=100\) s to \(t=120\) s (no further gas is produced), so the reaction has stopped by \(t = 100\) s — one of the reactants (here, the marble chips, since acid is in excess) has been completely used up.

**(c) Why rate decreases over time.** As the reaction proceeds, the concentration of hydrochloric acid decreases (it is being used up, even though it starts in excess). A lower acid concentration means fewer acid particles per unit volume, so the frequency of effective collisions between acid particles and the calcium carbonate surface decreases, which lowers the rate of reaction. This is why the volume-time graph is steepest at the start and gradually levels off to a horizontal line once the reaction is complete.

**Common mistake:** calculating "average rate" as total volume ÷ total time from \(t=0\) (i.e. \(72/120 = 0.6\) cm³ s⁻¹) when the question asks for the rate over a specific interval — always use the *change* in volume across the *stated* interval, not the values from the start of the experiment.

## Answer

(a) The average rate of reaction between \(t=20\) s and \(t=60\) s is **0.8 cm³ s⁻¹**.

(b) The reaction has stopped by **\(t = 100\) s**, since the volume of gas no longer changes (72 cm³ at both 100 s and 120 s) — one reactant (the marble chips) is fully used up.

(c) Rate decreases because acid concentration falls as the reaction proceeds, reducing the frequency of effective collisions between acid and carbonate particles.
