## Question

Excess small marble chips (calcium carbonate) are reacted with 50 cm³ of 0.2 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid. The volume of carbon dioxide gas collected is recorded every 10 seconds:

| Time / s | 0 | 10 | 20 | 30 | 40 | 50 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume of CO₂ / cm³ | 0 | 60 | 96 | 114 | 120 | 120 |

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction between \(t = 0\) s and \(t = 20\) s, in cm³/s.

(b) State the time at which the reaction is complete, giving a reason from the data.

(c) Show, by calculation, that the total volume of gas collected is consistent with the amount of hydrochloric acid used. (1 mole of gas occupies 24000 cm³ at r.t.p.)

## Solution

**Step 1 — (a) Average rate between \(t=0\) and \(t=20\) s.**

$$\text{average rate} = \frac{\Delta V}{\Delta t} = \frac{96 - 0}{20 - 0} = 4.8 \text{ cm}^3/\text{s}$$

**Step 2 — (b) Time reaction is complete.**

The volume of gas stops increasing between \(t=40\) s (120 cm³) and \(t=50\) s (120 cm³) — no further gas is produced, so the reaction is complete at **\(t = 40\) s**. (Marble chips are in excess, so the reaction stops because the hydrochloric acid, the limiting reagent, has been fully used up.)

**Step 3 — (c) Check the total gas volume against the moles of HCl.**

$$\text{CaCO}_3(s) + 2\text{HCl}(aq) \rightarrow \text{CaCl}_2(aq) + \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) + \text{CO}_2(g)$$

Moles of HCl used:

$$n(\text{HCl}) = \frac{50}{1000} \times 0.2 = 0.01 \text{ mol}$$

From the equation, mole ratio HCl : CO₂ = 2 : 1, so:

$$n(\text{CO}_2) = \frac{0.01}{2} = 0.005 \text{ mol}$$

$$V(\text{CO}_2) = 0.005 \times 24000 = 120 \text{ cm}^3$$

This matches the final volume of 120 cm³ in the table, confirming the data is consistent with the acid being fully used up (the limiting reagent).

**Common mistake:** for part (a), dividing the volume at \(t=20\) s by the *total* elapsed time from the start of the whole table (e.g. using 50 s) instead of the actual interval asked for (0 s to 20 s); and for part (b), picking \(t=50\) s as "reaction complete" instead of the earlier time (\(t=40\) s) at which the volume first stops changing.

## Answer

(a) 4.8 cm³/s. (b) The reaction is complete at \(t = 40\) s, since the gas volume stays at 120 cm³ from then on. (c) \(n(\text{CO}_2) = 0.005\) mol gives \(V = 120\) cm³, matching the table.
