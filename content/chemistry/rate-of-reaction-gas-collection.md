## Question

Excess marble chips (calcium carbonate) are reacted with dilute hydrochloric acid:

$$\text{CaCO}_3(s) + 2\text{HCl}(aq) \rightarrow \text{CaCl}_2(aq) + \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) + \text{CO}_2(g)$$

In the first 20 seconds of the reaction, 120 cm³ of CO₂ gas is collected, measured at room temperature and pressure (r.t.p.).

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction over this period in cm³/s.

(b) Given that the molar volume of a gas at r.t.p. is 24 000 cm³/mol, calculate the average rate of reaction in mol/s.

## Solution

**Part (a).** Average rate is the volume of gas produced divided by the time taken:

$$\text{rate} = \frac{\text{volume of CO}_2}{\text{time}} = \frac{120\text{ cm}^3}{20\text{ s}} = 6\text{ cm}^3/\text{s}$$

**Part (b).** First convert the volume of CO₂ collected into moles, using the molar volume at r.t.p.:

$$n(\text{CO}_2) = \frac{120\text{ cm}^3}{24\,000\text{ cm}^3/\text{mol}} = 0.005\text{ mol}$$

Then divide by the time to get the rate in mol/s:

$$\text{rate} = \frac{0.005\text{ mol}}{20\text{ s}} = 2.5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ mol/s}$$

**Check.** Converting back: \(2.5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ mol/s} \times 24\,000\text{ cm}^3/\text{mol} = 6\text{ cm}^3/\text{s}\), which matches the answer to part (a), confirming both values are consistent.

**Common mistake:** using the molar volume as 24 dm³/mol without converting it to 24 000 cm³/mol to match the units of the gas volume (120 cm³) — mixing dm³ and cm³ gives an answer that is out by a factor of 1000.

## Answer

(a) The average rate of reaction is **6 cm³/s**.

(b) The average rate of reaction is **2.5 × 10⁻⁴ mol/s**.
