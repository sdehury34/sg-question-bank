## Question

Excess marble chips (calcium carbonate) are reacted with dilute hydrochloric acid in a flask on a mass balance. The flask loses mass as \(\text{CO}_2\) gas escapes. In the first 40 seconds of the reaction, the flask's mass falls by 2.5 g.

(a) Calculate the average rate of reaction in g s⁻¹.

(b) Calculate the average rate of reaction in mol s⁻¹.

(c) Hence calculate the average rate at which \(\text{CO}_2\) gas is produced, in cm³ s⁻¹, at room temperature and pressure (r.t.p.), where 1 mole of gas occupies 24000 cm³.

[\(A_r\): C = 12, O = 16]

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rate in g s⁻¹ (part a).** Mass lost is entirely \(\text{CO}_2\) escaping, so:

$$\text{rate} = \frac{\text{mass lost}}{\text{time}} = \frac{2.5 \text{ g}}{40 \text{ s}} = 0.0625 \text{ g s}^{-1}$$

**Step 2 — Convert to mol s⁻¹ (part b).** \(M_r(\text{CO}_2) = 12 + 2(16) = 44\).

Total moles of \(\text{CO}_2\) lost:

$$n = \frac{2.5}{44} = 0.056818 \text{ mol}$$

Rate in mol s⁻¹:

$$\frac{0.056818}{40} = 0.0014205 \approx 1.42 \times 10^{-3} \text{ mol s}^{-1} \ (\text{3 s.f.})$$

**Step 3 — Convert to a gas volume rate (part c).** At r.t.p., 1 mole of gas occupies 24000 cm³, so total volume of \(\text{CO}_2\) produced:

$$V = 0.056818 \times 24000 = 1363.6 \text{ cm}^3$$

Rate in cm³ s⁻¹:

$$\frac{1363.6}{40} = 34.09 \approx 34.1 \text{ cm}^3\text{s}^{-1} \ (\text{3 s.f.})$$

**Check.** Working directly from the mol s⁻¹ rate: \(1.4205\times10^{-3} \times 24000 = 34.1\) cm³ s⁻¹, which matches. ✓

**Common mistake:** using the molar gas volume for STP (22400 cm³ mol⁻¹) instead of r.t.p. (24000 cm³ mol⁻¹) — the two are only interchangeable if the question specifies the same conditions, and the O-Level syllabus uses 24 dm³ mol⁻¹ (24000 cm³ mol⁻¹) at r.t.p.

## Answer

(a) \(0.0625\) g s⁻¹

(b) \(1.42 \times 10^{-3}\) mol s⁻¹ (3 s.f.)

(c) \(34.1\) cm³ s⁻¹ (3 s.f.)
