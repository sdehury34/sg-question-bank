## Question

Calcium carbonate decomposes on strong heating according to the equation:

$$\text{CaCO}_3 \rightarrow \text{CaO} + \text{CO}_2$$

Calculate the mass of carbon dioxide produced when 10.0 g of calcium carbonate is completely decomposed.

(Relative atomic masses: Ca = 40, C = 12, O = 16)

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the Mr of CaCO₃.**

$$M_r(\text{CaCO}_3) = 40 + 12 + (3 \times 16) = 100$$

**Step 2 — Find the number of moles of CaCO₃.**

$$n(\text{CaCO}_3) = \frac{\text{mass}}{M_r} = \frac{10.0}{100} = 0.100\ \text{mol}$$

**Step 3 — Use the mole ratio.** From the equation, 1 mol CaCO₃ produces 1 mol CO₂, so

$$n(\text{CO}_2) = 0.100\ \text{mol}$$

**Step 4 — Convert to mass.** \(M_r(\text{CO}_2) = 12 + (2 \times 16) = 44\)

$$\text{mass of CO}_2 = 0.100 \times 44 = 4.40\ \text{g}$$

**Common mistake:** using the Mr of CaCO₃ (100) instead of CO₂ (44) in the final step.

## Answer

Mass of carbon dioxide produced = **4.40 g**
