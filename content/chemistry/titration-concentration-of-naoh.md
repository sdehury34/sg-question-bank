## Question

In a titration, 25.0 cm³ of sodium hydroxide solution is exactly neutralised by 20.0 cm³ of hydrochloric acid of concentration 0.150 mol/dm³.

$$\text{NaOH} + \text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{NaCl} + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

Calculate the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution in mol/dm³.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Moles of HCl used.** Convert the volume to dm³: \(20.0\ \text{cm}^3 = 0.0200\ \text{dm}^3\).

$$n(\text{HCl}) = \text{concentration} \times \text{volume} = 0.150 \times 0.0200 = 0.00300\ \text{mol}$$

**Step 2 — Use the mole ratio.** From the equation, NaOH and HCl react in a 1 : 1 ratio, so

$$n(\text{NaOH}) = 0.00300\ \text{mol}$$

**Step 3 — Find the concentration of NaOH.** Volume of NaOH \(= 25.0\ \text{cm}^3 = 0.0250\ \text{dm}^3\).

$$\text{concentration} = \frac{n}{V} = \frac{0.00300}{0.0250} = 0.120\ \text{mol/dm}^3$$

**Common mistake:** forgetting to convert cm³ to dm³ (divide by 1000) before using \(n = cV\).

## Answer

Concentration of sodium hydroxide = **0.120 mol/dm³**
