## Question

In a redox titration, 25.0 cm³ of 0.0200 mol/dm³ acidified potassium manganate(VII), KMnO₄, reacts completely with 20.0 cm³ of iron(II) sulfate solution according to the equation:

$$\text{MnO}_4^- + 5\text{Fe}^{2+} + 8\text{H}^+ \rightarrow \text{Mn}^{2+} + 5\text{Fe}^{3+} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O}$$

(a) Identify the oxidising agent and the reducing agent, explaining your answer in terms of oxidation states.

(b) Calculate the concentration, in mol/dm³, of the iron(II) sulfate solution.

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify oxidation state changes.**

In \(\text{MnO}_4^-\), manganese has oxidation state \(+7\); in \(\text{Mn}^{2+}\) it is \(+2\). Manganese's oxidation state decreases, so \(\text{MnO}_4^-\) is **reduced** — it is the **oxidising agent**.

In \(\text{Fe}^{2+}\), iron has oxidation state \(+2\); in \(\text{Fe}^{3+}\) it is \(+3\). Iron's oxidation state increases, so \(\text{Fe}^{2+}\) is **oxidised** — it is the **reducing agent**.

**Step 2 — Calculate moles of MnO₄⁻ used.**

$$n(\text{MnO}_4^-) = c \times V = 0.0200 \text{ mol/dm}^3 \times \frac{25.0}{1000}\text{ dm}^3 = 5.00 \times 10^{-4}\text{ mol}$$

**Step 3 — Use the mole ratio from the balanced equation.**

From the equation, \(\text{MnO}_4^- : \text{Fe}^{2+} = 1 : 5\), so:

$$n(\text{Fe}^{2+}) = 5 \times 5.00\times10^{-4} = 2.50 \times 10^{-3}\text{ mol}$$

**Step 4 — Calculate the concentration of Fe²⁺.**

$$c(\text{Fe}^{2+}) = \frac{n}{V} = \frac{2.50\times10^{-3}\text{ mol}}{20.0/1000\text{ dm}^3} = \frac{2.50\times10^{-3}}{0.0200} = 0.125\text{ mol/dm}^3$$

**Check:** working backwards, \(0.125 \text{ mol/dm}^3 \times 0.0200\text{ dm}^3 = 2.50\times10^{-3}\text{ mol Fe}^{2+}\); dividing by 5 gives \(5.00\times10^{-4}\text{ mol MnO}_4^-\), which matches Step 2 exactly.

**Common mistake:** assuming a 1:1 mole ratio between \(\text{MnO}_4^-\) and \(\text{Fe}^{2+}\) instead of reading the 1:5 ratio off the balanced equation, or forgetting to convert cm³ to dm³ before dividing.

## Answer

Oxidising agent: \(\text{MnO}_4^-\); reducing agent: \(\text{Fe}^{2+}\).

Concentration of iron(II) sulfate solution \(= 0.125\text{ mol/dm}^3\).
